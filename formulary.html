Acne Vulgaris
Inflammatory and non-inflammatory acne. Treatment stepwise from topical agents through to combination oral therapy. Oral isotretinoin excluded from formulary — refer to specialist if indicated.
NICE NG198 BAD Acne Guidelines 2021

Rosacea
Chronic facial skin condition with erythematotelangiectatic, papulopustular, phymatous, and ocular subtypes. Treatment depends on subtype and severity.
BAD Rosacea Guidelines 2021 NICE CKS Rosacea
DrugFormulationDose / RegimenClinical NotesGuideline Ref
Metronidazole
metronidazole 0.75%
 0.75% gel0.75% cream Apply twice daily to affected areas First-line topical for papulopustular rosacea. Reduces inflammation and pustules. Continue for at least 8 weeks. BAD 2021
Azelaic Acid
azelaic acid 15%
 15% gel (Finacea) Apply twice daily Alternative to metronidazole. Effective for papulopustules and erythema. Safe in pregnancy. BAD 2021
Ivermectin
ivermectin 10mg/g
 1% cream (Soolantra) Apply once daily for up to 4 months For moderate–severe papulopustular rosacea. Anti-inflammatory and anti-Demodex activity. Superior to metronidazole in some trials. BAD 2021
Brimonidine

Doxycycline
doxycycline 40mg (sub-antimicrobial)
 40mg MR capsule (Efracea) 40mg once daily for up to 16 weeks Sub-antimicrobial dose — anti-inflammatory, not antibiotic effect. First-line oral for moderate–severe papulopustular rosacea. BAD 2021
Eczema (Atopic Dermatitis)
Chronic inflammatory skin condition. Management is stepwise: emollients form the basis of all treatment; topical corticosteroids and calcineurin inhibitors added based on severity.
NICE NG190 BAD Atopic Eczema Guidelines
DrugFormulationDose / RegimenClinical NotesGuideline Ref
Emollients
various (e.g. Epaderm, Diprobase, Cetraben)
 creamointmentlotion Apply liberally and frequently — at least twice daily and after washing Cornerstone of all eczema management. Choice based on patient preference. Ointments more occlusive; creams for daytime use. NICE NG190
Hydrocortisone
hydrocortisone 1%
 1% cream1% ointment Apply once or twice daily to affected areas; use sparingly Mild TCS. First-line for mild eczema and face/flexures/genitalia. Not for prolonged use. Safe for children. NICE NG190
Betamethasone Valerate
betamethasone valerate 0.1%
 0.1% cream0.1% ointment Apply once daily to affected areas for up to 4 weeks Moderately potent TCS. For moderate eczema on body (not face). Avoid prolonged use; risk of skin thinning. NICE NG190
Mometasone Furoate
mometasone furoate 0.1%
 0.1% cream0.1% ointment Apply once daily to affected areas Potent TCS. Once-daily dosing improves adherence. Avoid face. For moderate–severe body eczema. BAD Guidelines
Tacrolimus
tacrolimus 0.1%
 0.1% ointment (Protopic) Apply twice daily; reduce to once daily when controlled Topical calcineurin inhibitor. Second-line; steroid-sparing. Suitable for face and flexures. Not for infected eczema. Burning sensation initially common. NICE NG190
Pimecrolimus
pimecrolimus 1%
 1% cream (Elidel) Apply twice daily to affected areas Mild–moderate calcineurin inhibitor. Suitable for sensitive areas including face. Less effective than tacrolimus for severe disease. NICE NG190

Psoriasis
Chronic immune-mediated inflammatory skin condition. This formulary covers mild–moderate plaque psoriasis manageable in a remote private dermatology setting. Severe, erythrodermic, or pustular psoriasis requires face-to-face specialist care.
NICE NG137 BAD Psoriasis Guidelines

Seborrhoeic Dermatitis
Chronic inflammatory condition affecting sebaceous gland-rich areas (scalp, face, chest). Associated with Malassezia yeast. Tends to relapse and remit — treatment is primarily maintenance-based.
BAD Seborrhoeic Dermatitis Guidelines NICE CKS Seborrhoeic Dermatitis
DrugFormulationDose / RegimenClinical NotesGuideline Ref
Ketoconazole
ketoconazole 2%
 2% shampoo2% cream Shampoo: twice weekly for 4 weeks, then once weekly maintenance. Cream: apply once or twice daily. First-line antifungal. Effective against Malassezia. Shampoo for scalp; cream for face and body. Maintenance therapy prevents relapse. NICE CKS
Selenium Sulphide
selenium sulphide 2.5%
 2.5% shampoo Apply to scalp, leave 2–3 minutes, rinse; twice weekly for 2 weeks then weekly Antifungal and cytostatic. Alternative to ketoconazole shampoo. Can discolour light hair — advise patient. BAD Guidelines
Hydrocortisone
hydrocortisone 1%
 1% cream Apply twice daily for up to 2 weeks to reduce inflammation Mild TCS for acute flares on face or body. Not for long-term use. Do not use as monotherapy — combine with antifungal where possible. NICE CKS
Ketoconazole + Hydrocortisone
ketoconazole 2% / hydrocortisone 1%
 cream (Daktacort) Apply twice daily for up to 2 weeks Combination antifungal + mild TCS for inflamed seborrhoeic dermatitis on face and body. Short-term use only. BAD Guidelines
Clinical Judgement and Disclaimer: This formulary represents approved treatment options for Lyvia Health Ltd and is aligned to current NICE guidelines and BAD recommendations at the time of publication. It does not replace individual clinical assessment. The prescribing clinician retains full responsibility for determining the appropriateness of any treatment for an individual patient, including reviewing contraindications, drug interactions, and patient-specific risk factors. This formulary is subject to annual review. For treatments outside this formulary or where specialist input is required, the clinician should refer appropriately.
